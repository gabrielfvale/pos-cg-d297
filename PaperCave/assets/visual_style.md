# Paper Cave — Visual Identity Guide

## General Aesthetic

Paper Cave experiences follow a **sci-fi educational** aesthetic: futuristic research
environments, digital laboratories, holographic interfaces. The tone is sophisticated
and technical, not playful.

Visual references: hard sci-fi spacecraft interiors (Arrival, Interstellar, Ex Machina),
JARVIS-style data analysis interfaces, futuristic research center architecture.

---

## Color Palette

### Primary Colors
- Luminous blue:   #00D4FF  — holograms, active elements, data in motion
- Metallic white:  #E8F0FE  — neutral surfaces, text, structures
- Dark blue:       #0A1628  — backgrounds, empty space, inactive areas

### Colors by Object Category
| Category     | Color           | Hex     | Use                              |
|--------------|-----------------|---------|----------------------------------|
| contribution | golden amber    | #FFB800 | maximum emphasis, central object |
| method       | cyan blue       | #00D4FF | processes, algorithms, pipelines |
| problem      | coral red       | #FF4444 | tension, obstacle, failure state |
| metric       | neon green      | #00FF88 | data, measurement, success       |
| limitation   | bluish grey     | #4A5568 | reduced opacity, unstable feel   |
| dataset      | luminous purple | #8B5CF6 | collections, sets, corpora       |
| result       | bright white    | #FFFFFF | max emission, revelation moment  |
| artifact     | cyan blue       | #00D4FF | same as method                   |
| character    | metallic white  | #E8F0FE | neutral, human presence          |
| relation     | luminous blue   | #00D4FF | connections, flows               |

### Never Use
- Brown, burnt orange, earthy tones
- Saturated children's-game colors (lime green, hot pink)
- Complex gradients — prefer solid colors with emission

---

## Materials and Finishes

- Metal surfaces: always light (aluminium, titanium) — never rusted or worn
- Glass: slightly blue-tinted, emissive edges
- Emission: used sparingly to highlight active or important objects
- Opaque surfaces: matte with slight specular highlight

---

## Typography

- Font: geometric sans-serif (Orbitron, Exo 2, or any technical/futuristic font)
- Main text: white on dark background
- Labels: luminous blue or white at 80% opacity
- Avoid serif or handwritten fonts

---

## Lighting

- Ambient: dark blue, low intensity
- Light sources: point lights, colored (blue, white, golden for contribution)
- Clean, readable lighting — no dramatic horror-game shadows

---

## Scale and Proportion

- contribution object: larger and more central, scale 1.5-2x others
- method and problem: medium scale, surrounding the central object
- metric and limitation: smaller scale, peripheral
- Spacing: generous — environment should not feel cluttered

---

## Particle Effects

- Use only for dynamic processes (data flow, transmission, computation)
- Color: always within palette (blue, white, golden)
- Density: low to medium — never excessive
- Speed: smooth for stable processes; erratic for problems/limitations

---

## Interactive Charts

- Background: dark blue (#0A1628) with emissive blue borders
- Bars: color by category (neon green for metrics, cyan for methods)
- Labels: white, technical font
- Grid: thin lines, white at 20% opacity
- On hover: selected bar turns golden (#FFB800) with exact-value tooltip
